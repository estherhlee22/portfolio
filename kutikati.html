KUTIKATI

E-COMMERCE STORE

BRANDING & WEB DESIGN

APPROACH & PROCESS

Kutikati is an organic baby clothing line whose aim is to sell products that care for the health and well-being of both babies and the environment. While there are many competing baby clothing brands, Kutikati is able to set itself apart by focusing on the strength of the product itself.

After surveying and interviewing Kutikati’s target audience (mothers of infants), I quickly saw a common ground amongst young mothers— their greatest concern is the health and well-being of their child. In addition to this common factor, most interviewees mentioned that they always are in need of more time. With these two factors, I decided that Kutikati’s e-commerce store shouldn’t showcase too many options and steps. Kutikati’s products themselves are the solution to infant parents’ greatest concern, so I minimized the layout to show just what is necessary. With the focus on the images of the clothes, Kutikati’s e-commerce store allows for a smooth and fast shopping experience for busy mothers.

RESEARCH

I compiled a list of user stories and features after looking through various e-commerce sites. Typically, many clothing sites, especially ones for babies, have multiple subcategories and even within those subcategories, have options to specify their search. In my original list of user stories, I considered including these options in Kutikati’s site as well. However, given the company size of Kutikati, I decided that these options are not necessary and only complicate the experience of the users.

DESIGN

In designing Kutikati’s logo, I decided to create a text-based logo rather than an iconic one. The nature of Kutikati’s product focuses on a friendly tone, both to individual families and the environment, so I didn’t think it was necessary to to market itself as a massive enterprise with a strong iconic logo as many iconic logo-based clothing lines are. Instead, I opted for a clean and well typeset text-based logo that could easily go on tags and labels.

The color palette was drawn from classic baby colors, which include pastel hues such as the baby pink and beige. In addition, I wanted to highlight the organic aspect of the brand through the forest green. The orange was brought in to create an accent in the type. The combination of the colors create a synergy of the playful tone of Kutikati’s clothing.
